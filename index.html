WHAT IS LITTERING?

Littering is an increasingly prevalent bad habit of thoughtlessly throwing away or leaving rubbish lying around in public places instead of using the rubbish bins or wastepaper baskets provided. In Singapore, it is noted that most litterbugs were caught discarding cigarette butts, tissue paper, cigarette box wrappers and plastic cups inappropriately. So how often do such acts take place in Singapore?

More than 31,000 fines issued in 2016.

More than 1,700 corrective work orders (CWOs) imposed in 2016.

Estimated 200kg to 400kg of litter retrieved from the Singapore River daily.


36.2% of respondents in a 2010 study may litter if they believe they would not be caught.

541 enforcement actions taken against persons caught for high-rise littering in 2014.







HOW DOES THAT AFFECT ME?


Accidents

Passers-by are susceptible to injuries or even deaths due to high-rise litter. The elderly and children are especially at risk of slip and fall as a result of litter on the ground.

Health and Well-Being

Toxic chemicals released from litter, such as cigarette butts, can pollute the environment (land and air) and can lead to cancer, immune system problems and even birth defects when they are transferred to our bodies.

Animals

Marine animals or seabirds and even domestic pets will be poisoned by litter with chemical composition or get killed as their digestive systems are filled with trash, which may lead to extinction of species.

Flooding

Litter that is washed into the common drain can result in clogged drainage which can cause floods. This would also result in illnesses and diseases if oblivious persons drink the unfiltered water.

Pests

Litter can attract pests such as cockroaches and mice to residential areas, and may also create breeding grounds. Such pests allow diseases to spread more easily which will affect residents living there.
